How It Works

Architecture, spam detection, and how the filter gets smarter over time

The Architecture: IMAP Transport → Local Storage → Analysis

GravesMail uses your email provider’s IMAP server purely as a transport layer. I never read from IMAP to display messages — that would be slow and unreliable. Instead, here’s what happens:

  1. Fetch from IMAP — Messages are downloaded from your provider’s server over an encrypted connection.
  2. Stage in DMZ — New messages land in a local staging area (SQLite) before you see them.
  3. 5-layer spam analysis — Every message runs through all five detection engines on your machine.
  4. Quick Rules filtering — Messages matching your personal Quick Rules are quarantined automatically.
  5. Promote to Inbox or Spam — Clean messages reach your Inbox. Threats go to Spam.
  6. Delete from server (optional) — If you’ve enabled “Delete from server,” the message is removed from your provider.

SQLite is the single source of truth. Everything you see in the UI comes from your local database, not from IMAP.

Two-Phase Sync for Large Mailboxes

When you first connect an account — especially one with thousands of messages — GravesMail uses a two-phase approach:

Phase 1 (Quick Start): The newest 500 messages per folder are fetched first, typically in under a minute. You can start reading and working immediately.

Phase 2 (Background Sync): The remaining messages download quietly in the background. A progress indicator in the status bar shows completion percentage and estimated time remaining. You can pause, resume, or stop at any time. Progress is saved if you close the app.

The 5-Layer Spam Detection Engine

Every incoming message passes through five independent detection engines. Each engine examines a different aspect of the message, and their weighted scores combine into a final spam verdict:

🧠

1. Bayesian Filter (30%)

Statistical analysis trained on 1,645 spam samples with 82,000+ tokens. Learns from your corrections over time — every “Mark as Junk” or “Not Spam” makes it smarter.

🌐

2. Domain Validator (25%)

DNS verification, MX record checks, and SPF validation. Catches messages from nonexistent or misconfigured domains.

🔒

3. Header Analyzer (15%)

SPF/DKIM/DMARC authentication and header anomaly detection. Identifies forged sender addresses and suspicious routing.

🔍

4. Heuristic Scorer (20%)

18 pattern-matching rules that detect phishing URLs, urgency language, suspicious formatting, and other common attack tactics.

🛡

5. Defender Integration (10%)

On Windows, messages are scanned by Windows Defender for known malware signatures. Other platforms use the remaining four engines at adjusted weights.

External Image Blocking & Tracking Pixel Stripping

When you receive an email, senders can embed external images that load from their servers. When the image loads, the sender learns your IP address, that you opened the email, when you opened it, and what device you used.

GravesMail blocks external images by default. Separately, it detects and strips invisible tracking pixels (1×1 images used solely for read-receipt tracking). These are independent controls — you can load images without loading trackers, or vice versa.

Quick Rules: Your Personal Email Triage

Quick Rules let you create one-click quarantine rules for specific senders, domains, or subject patterns. Each rule gets its own toolbar button. When you apply a rule to a message, the sender is automatically added to that rule’s match list — the rule grows smarter as you use it.

Rules can be set to auto-apply during sync, so matching messages are quarantined before they ever reach your Inbox. You can scan entire folders in seconds to bulk-quarantine old messages. Quarantined messages can be reviewed, restored, or purged at any time.

Automatic Encrypted Backups

GravesMail automatically creates encrypted backups of your email database:

  • Full backups — weekly, complete database copy, AES-256 encrypted
  • Differential backups — daily, only changes since last full backup
  • Pre-operation snapshots — before any destructive operation (Quick Rules scan, purge, clear trash)

All backups are encrypted with a machine-specific key and run silently in the background. The Backup Manager lets you browse any backup’s contents, search across all backups, compare with your current database, and selectively recover individual messages.

Deletion Log & Recovery

Every deleted message is captured in a local deletion log with full body content. You can browse, search, and one-click recover deleted messages for up to 90 days (configurable). No backup file needed — recovery is instant from the deletion log.

Community Spam Intelligence (Opt-In)

If you choose to participate, GravesMail can share anonymous spam pattern data — just word-frequency scores, never email content — to help improve the filter for all users. This data is combined into a shared baseline that ships with each new version, so new users start with the combined knowledge of the community.

Think of it like a neighborhood watch

If one neighbor sees something suspicious, they tell everyone else. Now the whole neighborhood knows what to look for. Nobody has to share their home address or daily routine — they just share what they saw.

It’s Your Choice

Sharing is completely optional. It’s turned off by default. You can turn it on or off at any time in Settings. If you don’t participate, your personal filter still learns and improves on its own. You still benefit from the shared baseline that shipped with your version.
